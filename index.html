What we produce

Events that earn their place in your calendar.

Two focused formats. Both designed for the same outcome: conversations that create lasting value for scientists and vendors alike.

On-site trade shows

Hosted at biotech and pharma campuses, incubator hubs, and universities across the Bay Area, these events are built around skilled matchmaking. We invite the right vendors to the right institutions, bringing the latest products, services, and technologies directly to the researchers who need them. We ensure that the hosting organizations walk away with something they can point to: new technologies discovered, new partnerships formed, and a team that left feeling their time was well spent.

Community & networking events

Open to the broader Bay Area life science community, these events share the same goals as our on-site trade shows while expanding the reach. Vendors connect with a wider pool of scientists and procurement professionals, and networking happens on a larger scale. Some events take on a more social character, like our BioBeats series, which brings the community together over live music and the kind of relaxed conversations that rarely happen in a conference room.

Our legacy

Two decades in the making.

Bio-EV didn't emerge from a pitch deck. It grew from years of showing up at labs, at trade shows, at conferences, and understanding what scientists and vendors actually need from each other.

2004The
foundation

A gap in the life science industry worth closing.

During the Bay Area's biotech boom, a clear need was emerging. Scientists and the vendors serving them rarely had meaningful opportunities to connect. The trade show model of the era was transactional, rushed, and poorly matched. The work of building something better began here, event by event, relationship by relationship.

2015The
evolution

Bio-EV is established. Alfred and Julie join forces.

By 2015, both founders had earned their perspective the hard way. Julie had spent years as a successful biotech sales professional before transitioning into life science events in 2008, bringing a vendor-side fluency that proved invaluable. Together, she and Alfred founded Bio-EV to raise the bar on what a life science event could be: more curated, more purposeful, and built to create value long after the room emptied.

TodayThe
network

A premier hub for life science collaboration.

From biotech and pharma companies to incubator hubs and universities across the SF Bay Area and beyond, Bio-EV is the trusted connector between scientists, vendors, and procurement teams. A community built not on volume, but on trust earned over time.

Meet Alfred & Julie

The people behind Bio-EV.

Julie Neeb

CEO

Julie has a rare gift for knowing who should be in the room together and making sure they get there. With roots in biotech sales and over fifteen years in life science events, she brings a vendor-side fluency to Bio-EV that shapes everything from how events are curated to how relationships are maintained long after the show ends. She is known for her attention to detail, her warmth, and her ability to make both scientists and sales teams feel equally well served. Julie lives in the San Francisco Bay Area with her husband and daughter and recharges on a yoga mat, a tennis court, a golf course, or out on the water.

Alfred Lagaya

President

Alfred's path to Bio-EV began in a research lab. After earning his Biology degree from San Diego State University, he worked as a scientist in genomic research during the Bay Area's biotech boom of the 1990s. A successful career in life science sales followed, covering everything from lab consumables to antibodies and custom services. He then spent over a decade shaping how scientists and vendors connect before establishing Bio-EV with Julie in 2015. He lives in the Bay Area with his wife and three kids, and outside of work he trains in Jiu Jitsu, plays golf, and has been DJing since before biotech was even a thing.
